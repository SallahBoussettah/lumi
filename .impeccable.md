## Design Context

### Users
A developer/creator on Linux who runs this app all day in the background. It watches their screen, listens to conversations, extracts memories and tasks. The user glances at it periodically — it's not the primary focus, it's a companion. Used in long sessions, often at night alongside dark terminal environments.

### Brand Personality
Perceptive, trustworthy, unhurried.

### Emotional Goals
When you open Omniscient, you should feel: "Everything is captured. Everything is organized. I can trust this." Not excitement, not urgency — quiet confidence. Like opening a beautifully kept personal journal.

### Aesthetic Direction
**"The Quiet Archive"** — a Moleskine notebook that writes itself.

- Warm dark backgrounds — charcoal paper, not void-black. Dark like a reading room at night, not like outer space.
- Typography-driven — text is the primary visual element. Beautiful fonts with real hierarchy and generous spacing.
- Minimal chrome — no unnecessary containers, borders, or decorative elements. Content floats in warm darkness.
- Single warm accent color — amber/ochre, like candlelight on paper. Used sparingly for active states and key actions only.
- Generous negative space — the emptiness is intentional. Let content breathe.
- No AI aesthetic clichés — no purple gradients, no neon glows, no glassmorphism, no border-left stripes.

### Anti-references
- Generic dark mode with purple/cyan accents (every AI product in 2024-2025)
- Dashboard UIs with cards-in-cards and decorative sparklines
- Void-black backgrounds with neon glows
- "Developer tool" aesthetic

### References (feeling, not copying)
- The calm density of a well-typeset book
- The warmth of analog — leather, paper, ink
- Apps that feel quiet but powerful: Things 3, iA Writer, Linear (the restraint, not the specific style)

### Design Principles
1. **Warmth over cold** — every surface, every color should feel human, not machine.
2. **Silence is design** — empty space is not wasted space. Resist the urge to fill.
3. **Typography carries the weight** — if the fonts and spacing are right, you need almost nothing else.
4. **One accent, used rarely** — when color appears, it means something.
5. **Invisible until needed** — the interface should recede, letting your captured life be the focus.
